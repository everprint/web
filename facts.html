Processing Steps for making Everprint Pre-inked stamps

Back to Top
Step Processing Time Required
1. Print out the wording on Vellum Paper (Tracing Paper) for stamp making 10 Seconds
2. Place the Vellum Paper on the machine's glass and place the OH film on top of the Vellum Paper. Place the flash pad on top of OH film and close the lid (Machine Cover) and the flash button to burn the flash pad. 10 seconds
3. Open the lid (Machine Cover) and examine the pad to ensure that the image is nicely burned on the flash pad. Use the sealing pen to seal up the spot incured by the dust if necessary. 30 seconds / 3 minutes
4. Put the inked cartridge on the stamp mount, lay the flash pad on the inked cartridge and use the clip to lock the flash pad onto the stamp mount. 10 seconds
5. Drop a few drops of ink on the letters/images so that the flash pad will absorb ink. 1 minute
6. Clean the access ink by stamping on the draft paper until have a clear print. 10 seconds
7. Indexing the stamp on the indicated label and mount it on the stamp mount. 10 seconds
8. Pack the stamp for delivery 10 seconds
Total time spent to complete the whole processing of making stamp will be 5 minutes

Processing steps for making Self-inking stamps

Back to Top
Step Processing Time required
1. Print out the wording on Vellum Paper (Tracing Paper) for stamp making 10 seconds (0.17 minute)
2. Use Vellum Paper (tracing Paper) to expose the film by using the development liquid 6 seconds (0.1 minute)
3. Lay develop film in imagegix liquid 1 minutes
4. Drying film after imagaegix liquid 1 minutes
5.
  1. Lay the negative film on the glass of the Liquid Photo Polymer machine and put the cover film on the negative
  2. Use Danning Tape to make border
  3. Putting the Polymer inside the border
  4. Brow the air buboo inside the Polymer if any
  5. Lay the backing subspray sheet on top of the Polymer and lay the another cover sheet on top of the backing subspray sheet and close the machine lead to start exposure of the Polymer.
3 to 5 minutes
6. Expose the Polymer at the front and back 452 seconds (7.53 minutes)
7. Use the Wash Out Solution to wash the exposed Polymer 2 minutes
8. Lay the washed Polymer in the Tacking Power Water 700 seconds (11.67 minutes)
9. Drying the Rubber (Polymer) 1 minute
10. Cutting the Rubber (Polymer) and stick it into the stamp mount. 1 minute
Total time spent to complete the whole processing of making stamp will be 30.47 minutes


Comments: Pre-inked stamp system only take 5 to 10 minutes to complete the stamp and deliver to customer but Self-inking stamp system take 30 minutes. No skillful staff required to produce the Pre-inked stamp but Self-inked stamp need a skillful staff to do so. In this regards, the company will have cost saving on the time to produce the stamp and at no risk lose a skillful staff for making stamp and are able to deliver the stamp to customr on time.



Materials used for making the Pre-inked stamp are:

Back to Top

  1. Pre-inked Stamp mount
  2. FlashPad (rubber)
  3. Vellum Paper (Tracing Paper) (to used as film)
  4. OH film (put in between Flash pad and Vellum paper)
  5. Flashpad inks (Re-ink the stamp by dropping ink on the Flash Pad when running out of ink)



Materials used for making the Self-inked stamp are:

Back to Top

  1. Self-inking Stamp mount
  2. Inked cartridge (change a new Inked cartridge when running out of Inks)
  3. Vellum Paper (Tracing paper) ( to use making the Negatives-film)
  4. Liquid Polymer ( to make rubber )
  5. Daylight Negatives (film)
  6. Developer Liquid ( for developing film)
  7. Imagegix Liquid ( for developing film)
  8. Cover film ( place in between film and Polymer as well as the backing sheet and glass on machine
  9. Backing subspray sheet ( backing plastic)
  10. Danning Tape ( Adhesive Tape) (make borders to guard the overflowing of Liquid Polymer)
  11. Wash Out Solution
  12. Tacking Powder (Exposure Powder) ( hardening the rubber)



Note: Stamp cannot deliver to customers if any single item of the above materials are out of stock for both Pre-inked stamp and Self-inking stamp.

Conclusion: Materials used for making the pre-inked stamp are controllable and 100% accounted for. However, most of the consumable materials for making the self-inking stamp are hardly control the correct usage and 100% invoiced. Furthermore, the investment of using the Pre-inked stamp system is lower than the Self-inking stamp system.
